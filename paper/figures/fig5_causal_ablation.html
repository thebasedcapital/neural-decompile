Figure 5. Causal ablation: sparse circuit is necessary and sufficient

Figure 5. Four-way causal ablation of Head 21 K projection. Fisher discriminant ratio measures script-type clustering in activation space (72 prompts, 9 categories). Full weights (5.94) and sparse circuit (5.66) produce equivalent clustering — the 2.7% sparse circuit carries the full signal. Ablation (zeroing all weights) destroys classification entirely (Fisher = 0.00), proving causal necessity. Random weights (matched variance) produce weaker clustering (2.56), proving the specific learned weights — not mere architectural position — are required.
